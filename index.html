A Definition of Creativity

"The use of imagination or original ideas to create something." --Oxford Dictionary, Web 2017

Creativity is a sort of black-box craft that entrepreneurs must yield. While their ideas do not come about by divine inspiration, they sometimes work diligently to conceal their traces. On the other hand, we often draw subconscious inspiration from experiences past, things that at least once changed the way we looked at the world. If we are attentive enough in our careers, their may be times when we alone see two or many pieces that need to be put together and it is that change of perspective, that creativity, developed from a lifetime that makes us entrepreneurs.

A Definition of Entrepreneurship

"A person who organizes and manages any enterprise, especially a business..." --Forbes (Rethinking The Definition of 'Entrepreneur'), Web 2017

By its very nature, entrepreneurship is a continually redefining concept. This article approached the boom of self-labelling "entreprenerus" who were internet-born, beamy-eyed, youngsters just starting out in industry with little experience if at all. The author was rethinking their harsh initial judgements of these characters, suggesting to rather appreciate and support those whose ambition has been increasingly shifting our global economy. As an easy target, I was glad to read through this sobering article: the words of a business-person who has had their share of experience and staff and revenue streams to show for it. It is much easier to find the motivation to change the world with enough of an attitude than it is to see it through and like-minded people in my position need to hear it. Going out into the workforce, I must hold on to my ambitions and not blame my misfortune and a lack of finance or other external factors. While they may hold validity, it is up to me and me alone as an entrepneur to further my vision.

An Illustration

I was jealoues of the work at IDEO. I wish I could lead a team of that many people that consistently to tackle such high-level problems with such enthusiasm and most importantly fun. As the president of Spartan Hackers, I regularly deal with a team of 12 people give or take and whenever I am not around I study how I can best set my team up to succeed and it is tough. That tasteful mixture of asking, coaching, supporting, and delegating is awesome and I can't believe how easy they make it look. I wasn't surprised when I discovered that the mastermind behind them was nothing less than an engineer turned-desinger turned-professor from Stanford. I continue to study his techniques in management consulting and organizational design to employ in my very own team.

In all, this case study in particular is quite the gem.
