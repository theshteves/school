CREATIVITY QUEST 1 SUBMISSION

Meet the Man

For this project, you'll create a web page (like this one, but know that this one is just one example of the many possibilities of what you can create!). On the web page, you'll include:
  1. a photograph and a brief biography statement introducing yourself
  2. a definition of creativity
  3. a definition of entrepreneurship
  4. an example that you think illustrates the importance of creativity to/in entrepreneurship

Start this submission AFTER you have explored the readings, cases, and sparks for weeks 1 and 2 of class. You can also draw upon these materials in your Creativity Quest 1 web page. (In fact, please do draw upon them! For instance, you may include a definition of creativity from one of our readings, or use one of our class cases or sparks as your example.)

The goals of this project are to:
  • engage in some research and exploration
  • get a stronger sense of how creativity is understood, while recognizing it is understood differently in different contexts
  • be able to articulate a definition of entrepreneurship, while recognizing it is understood differently in different contexts
  • present an example that connects creativity to entrepreneurship
  • create a well-written, well-designed web page that includes text and images.

A Definition of Creativity

Your web page should include some well-written, well-crafted text. Your web page should include some well-chosen images.

When you include others' definitions of creativity and entrepreneurship, feel free to copy and paste direct quotes, but note that they are direct quotes, and do a bit of situating and explaining. (Don't just paste in other people's words without providing some introduction and explanation!)

Likewise, when you include your example, don't just stick it in; be sure to explain what it is and why you think it's a good example.

Your web page should include citations and links to materials that you are drawing from--for instance, if you're summarizing from or quoting from a magazine article or another web page, cite that source. If you're summarizing from or quoting from the week's readings, cite that source. If you're using another person's image or video in your web page, cite that source.

I don't care how you cite the source (APA, MLA, Chicago, AP style, etc.), but please do two things:
  1. cite all of the sources of your information (text, images, etc.), and
  2. cite your sources consistently. Almost every style guide requires author name, date of publication, title of page/publication, and where the piece was published.

A Definition of Entrepreneurship

If you've never created a web page before, I'd suggest using weebly (http://www.weebly.com; I created this page using weebly), wix (http://wix.com), or squarespace (http://squarespace.com/) -- all three allow you to create a basic web page (and embed photos, links to multimedia, and more) for free. All three will also allow you to publish or "host" your web page there, and output a URL you can share with others so they can access your web page. 

If you've created web pages before, feel free to use whatever platform and hosting space you prefer.

You should NOT have to pay to use a tool or host your web site! All of the sites listed above provide FREE templates, and you can post for FREE on all of them. (There might be moments where you are invited to upgrade or to establish a free account, but you should NOT have to do so to complete this submission.)

To turn in your work for Creativity Quest 1, you'll be submitting a Word document to D2L. In the Word doc, include your name and the URL to your web page. If you're not sure how to generate the URL for the web page you've created, consult the help feature for the site you've used, or email or message me.

Your Creativity Quest 1 Word doc with your URL included is due uploaded to the dropbox on D2L by noon on Friday, February 3. 

An Illustration

When you generate the link to your Creativity Quest 1 web page, you will be publishing it live on the web. This means it's a public document that, theoretically, anyone can access. 

If you include your full name on your Creativity Quest 1 web page and I web search your name (e.g., if I'm an internship coordinator looking to see what sort of online identity you have), your Creativity Quest web page will likely show up in the search results.

If you want to include your full name on/in your web page and you're happy with it living online as an example of your MSU-related work, that's fine. However, if you just want to use your initials or your first name, that's fine, too.
